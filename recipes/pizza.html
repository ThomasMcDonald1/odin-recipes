Pizza Recipe

History and Description of this Recipe:

Pizza was first created in Naples, Italy. The earliest name of this dish was documented around AD 997 in Gaeta.

The best pizzas have a crispy, bread crust, cheesy surface and tomato-based sauce. If you haven't tried pizza before, be sure to read on and make it yourself!

Ingredients needed to make pizza:

  • 3 3/5 cups of All-Purpose Flour
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons of Dry Yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups of Warm Water (not too hot or it will kill the yeast!)
  • 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons of ground salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of sugar
  • Tomato Sauce (store bought, unless you find another recipe to make it!)
  • Firm mozzarella cheese, grated
  • Toppings of your choice (Pepperoni, mushroom, garlic; The sky's the limit!)

Steps to make your pizza:

  1. Proof the yeast:

    Place the warm water in a large bowl and sprinkle in the yeast until dissolved.

    After a few minutes, you should notice a beige-colored foam around the surface of the mixture. This means the yeast are alive and active!

    If you do not see the foaming/bloom of the mixture, this means your yeast is either expired or dead (heat can kill yeast) and you may either want to try again or buy new yeast!

  2. Now that you have your bloomed yeast-water mixture, you can proceed to making the dough.

    Add the flour, sugar, salt, and olive oil and mix with either your hands or a mixing paddle.

    You'll know the mixture is finished once everything is uniform and a bit sticky. (no clumps should remain)

    If the mixture is too tough to move with your hands, add a bit more water.

    If the mixture is too watery, add a bit more flour.

  3. Let the Dough Rise

    Spread a thin amount of oil around a large bowl and place the dough you made within it. Cover the bowl with cling wrap and let it sit in room temperature for about an hour and a half (70-80 degrees Fahrenheight).

    This allows the yeast a bit of time to ferment and rises the dough.

  4. Knead a stretch your dough

    After your dough is finished rising, we can make 2 medium-sized pizzas! So divide your dough in 2 equal portions and make them round. Save the other for later, or repeat these steps to make 2 pizzas!

    Flatten/stretch the dough out until it is about half an inch thick all around.

    Place the flattened dough on a cooking surface (such as a cooking sheet with foil)

    Preheat your over to 475 degrees Fahrenheight, and we can begin placing toppings.

  5. Adding toppings

    Now that your dough is placed on a cooking surface, you'll begin adding toppings.

    First:

    1. Add a thin-layer of pizza sauce, but leave room for the outer crust
    2. Next, add the shredded mozarella cheese on top of the sauce enough to cover it up. Still leave room for the outer crust!
    3. Finally, add whatever toppings you like over the cheese! I tend to add pepperoni, onion, garlic, and mushrooms.
    4. Additionally, if you want your pizza to have more flavor, you can add italian seasoning spices with ground salt and peppercorn

  6. Cooking your pizza

    Now that our pizza has all its topping, it's time to cook!

    Place your pizza on the center rack of your pre-heated oven. It should be 475 degrees Fahrenheight!

    Close the over, and let it cook for about 12-15 minutes depending on how crispy and crunchy you want your crust!

  7. Enjoying your cooked pizza

    After the pizza has finished cooking, take it out the over and let it cool for about 7 minutes.

    Cut the pizza in slices using a pizza cutter, grab some slices, and enjoy!
